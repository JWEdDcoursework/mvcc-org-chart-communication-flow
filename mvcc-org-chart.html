Board of Trustees

Governing Body

Composition:

  • 5 Trustees appointed by Oneida County Executive
  • 4 Trustees appointed by the Governor
  • 1 Student Trustee (elected annually, 1-year term)
  • All other appointments: 7-year terms

Responsibilities:

  • Sets all college policies
  • Negotiates contracts with bargaining units
  • Approves college finances (budget and expenditures)
  • Approves appointments at most levels
  • Advocates to governmental agencies for the college

📢 Communication Flows

Communicates To Communicates Through
President Board of Trustee meetings
President's Cabinet Board of Trustees meetings
Mid-level Administration (AVPs, Deans, Directors) Official Board Communications (memos, policy statements) and occasional attendance at Board Meetings (School presentations at BOT workshops)
Faculty and Staff Board meeting minutes, Public addresses at college-wide events
Community/Local Legislature Presentations at county legislature meetings, public addresses at on and off campus events (capital improvement groundbreaking, public partnership launches)

President

Chief Executive and Administrative Officer

Reports to: Board of Trustees

Responsibilities:

  • Makes recommendations to Board of Trustees regarding all facets of academic programs and staffing
  • Executive decision-making authority as established by the Board
  • Overall leadership of the college

📢 Communication Flows

Communicates To Communicates Through
Board of Trustees Board of Trustee meetings
President's Cabinet Cabinet meetings
Mid-level Administration (AVPs, Deans, Directors) Administrator meetings, College update emails
Faculty and Staff Opening Day and Data Summit programs, College Senate attendance, College update emails
Community/Local Legislature Presentations at county legislature meetings
Students Attendance at Student Congress meetings, Personal interactions, Blog posts

VP of Student Affairs

Student Services Division

Oversees:

  • Admission
  • Enrollment
  • Holistic student support
  • Student engagement and development
  • Residence life (MVCC Dormitory Corporation)

Also serves as: Title IX and Title VI officer for the college

VP of Learning & Academic Affairs

Academic Division

Responsibilities:

  • Developing goals and objectives for academic programs
  • Planning and organizing instructional programs
  • Maintaining financial and staffing controls
  • Administering and evaluating both credit and non-credit instructional programs

Academic Deans & Schools

Department Leadership

Dean Responsibilities:

  • Administrate degree and certificate programs within their School
  • Manage course offerings
  • Evaluate faculty
  • Maintain course schedules and staffing
  • Assign teaching loads according to CBA guidelines
  • Ensure satisfactory academic progress, rigor, and integrity
  • Review transfer courses
  • Certify graduation for students

📢 Communication Flows

Communicates To Communicates Through
President Through AVPs to VPs
President's Cabinet Through AVPs
Mid-level Administration (AVPs, Deans, Directors) Deans Team meetings
Faculty and Staff School meetings, newsletters, word of mouth, classroom observations, individual emails
Students Through faculty, individual meetings, word of mouth, student concern forms
